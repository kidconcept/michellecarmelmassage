The Transformation

A massage shaped around what your body needs today.

$180-$350 sliding scale

The Transformation gives Michelle room to listen to what your body is asking for and shape the session around it. The work can move between calming full-body massage, focused attention to stubborn tissue, mobility-minded bodywork, and restorative pacing.

The goal is not to force your body through a preset routine. It is to create a clear, responsive arc from arrival to release to a quieter nervous system.

01 Listen

Start with the pattern, not a preset routine.

Michelle begins by listening to what is most present that day: the tight shoulder that keeps returning, the hips that feel guarded, the back that needs slower work, or the general fatigue that asks for calming full-body care.

That conversation shapes the pace, pressure, and focus before the bodywork begins.

02 Release

Give stubborn tissue enough time to respond.

Focused work gives extra attention to areas that need care without losing the feeling of a complete massage. Pressure can move from soothing to therapeutic and back again, so the session feels restorative rather than abrupt.

This is where tension patterns can soften, mobility can open, and the body can begin to feel more spacious.

03 Restore

Let the whole body settle before the session ends.

The final part of the massage brings the work back into the full body: slower transitions, grounding touch, and space for your nervous system to downshift.

You are not rushed off the table. The session is designed to close with the feeling of being genuinely taken care of.

Benefits

What a good session can help create.

Clients often choose massage when they want to soften tension, move with more ease, settle their nervous system, and feel more present in their body. Michelle's approach supports those benefits with attentive pressure, slow transitions, and a session that is never rushed.

  • Release everyday tension in the back, neck, shoulders, hips, legs, and feet.
  • Support mobility and range of motion through areas that feel tight or overworked.
  • Encourage the nervous system to move out of stress and into rest.
  • Promote circulation, relaxation, and a renewed sense of whole-body ease.

Who it's for

High-quality massage should be available to everyone.
